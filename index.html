"Dr. Muhammad Yunus, a Nobel Laureate and esteemed economist, is globally recognized for pioneering the concept of microfinance through the Grameen Bank, enabling millions of impoverished individuals, especially women, to achieve financial independence. As the Chief Adviser of the People's Republic of Bangladesh, he played a significant role in addressing pressing national challenges, emphasizing inclusive growth, sustainability, and innovation. In his vision for Bangladesh, Dr. Yunus highlighted the potential of the nation, stating, "Bangladesh can be a beacon of hope for the world by embracing sustainable development and empowering its people to create a poverty-free future." His leadership and inspiring ideas have elevated Bangladesh on the global stage, promoting equitable opportunities and envisioning a brighter, self-reliant future."

Dr. Muhammad Yunus
